Consider a used ereader

A few months ago I found myself in a pickle. I really wanted to read a book ,but I found myself totally priced out. And, unfortunately, the library was no help either. By some stroke of ahem luck, I was able to find it through other means.

Skill issues

So, I started reading it on my laptop. Here's where I encountered some issues. First of all, I found it quite unpleasant to read for long periods of time due to the laptop itself. I tend to curl up somewhat when I read a book, and my laptop really isn't compatible with that. My phone was the next viable option, and while that solved the comfort problem, it introduced a new one with the tiny screen. There were also two skill-issues problems endemic to both of these devices:

1) Distracting: on my phone in particular, I'd get a text or other notification every few minutes, and I'd just get sucked into that. I've since tried to reduce the number of non-essential notifs, but at the time it was pretty bad

2) Screen brightness: my impression is that the whole blue light thing is a bit overblown, but I was still not the biggest fan. It's just very bright, and something feels slightly wrong about it. (again, I'll admit that this is mostly a vibes based problem. I know people who are perfectly happy to read on their phones! If that's you, please stop reading this)

So at this point I was dissatisfied and leaning towards just not reading my precious philosophy text. However, I did find a solution I was happy with!

A cheap, used kindle

I really just bought the cheapest ereader I could find. There was a listing on goodwill auctions, and I just went for it. In my case, it ended up being $10 (well, I guess $20 with shipping) kindle 4. This thing is not fancy. It came out in 2011, still uses a micro USB port (ewwwww), and has no backlight. That said, I love it.

It does exactly what I need it to. Well sort of. Without Calibre, I don't think that I'd be having nearly as much fun. Seriously, this program is awesome and so so plug and play. With the power of calibre, I can sync news, rss feeds, epubs, mobis, the occasional PDF. It's great!

It's wonderful just being able to carry around so many books on one device. And for such low a cost of entry it's been easy to justify it to myself. For one, I think about all the money I'm saving per book. Sure, it's nice to have physical books. I won't argue with that; they look lovely on shelves and feel great in my hands. But I simply don't want to spend a minimum of ten dollars on books every 2 weeks/months. Even going to the library has a cost associated when I take into account gas. (sidenote: I would like to start using my library more, even though I do have an ereader! I think it's such a cool resource, and I like supporting things in my community when I can). It also seems like there might be a meager environmental argument for using an ereader, given the energy costs of producing paper. I don't put too much weight in this though.

Lastly, I think that it's really fun to read less conventional media on an eink display. I recently read some fanfiction as an epub, and it was a great experience! (well okay, the fanfiction was mid, but the medium was way better than a website). The same holds for blog posts, web serials, and even comics if they look alright in grayscale.

Don't buy a new one (probably)

I really want to emphasize that you shouldn't just consume and shop because it feels good. Only buy one if you share my gripes with phone/laptop reading, and think you'd actually use an ereader. I don't honestly think that having an ereader has made me read that much more. Sure, the convenience I've described is wonderful, but I reject the premise that you should buy an ereader and hope it will magically make you a reader. That's dumb. And frivolous purchases probably go against your interests.

But, if I have convinced you that there's some value here, buy a used one! I will admit, the kindle I purchased was pretty bear-bones. But honestly, I think this is exactly what you want! Modern kindles include many more distractions alongside all their features. I don't want ads on my reading device! I get enough of those everywhere else. There are some cool companies (kobo, for example) that make modern, and feature-rich ereaders, but I genuinely don't see the point. If your goal is just to read books, maybe in the dark, consider a used kindle voyage: the community seems to like it, and you can find them for $50 on ebay.

Since I started writing this, I ordered a used kindle 3. I'm a filthy consumer, I know, I know. But, it was also only $20, and now I'll have a physical keyboard (!!!) which I can use to take notes as I read. I'll add an update at the bottom if I have any more insights about this.

One last note, kindle jailbreaks

With such old devices on my hands, I don't really feel a need for these. I scarcely turn on the internet on my devices, since I can really do all the ebook management through Calibre. However, if you do find yourself with a newer kindle or just want more abilities to customize your ereader, you can! Just google around a bit, and you'll find all the information you need to install koreader, and buck the adware that comes installed on newer kindles.
